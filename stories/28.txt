I found my first job as a waiter. I had been a book publisher from the ages of 16 and 17 in Britain, so I had some experience. It is said, by some, that I once gave a bookseller a handkerchief and said, 'You have to write a lot of books, or it will never sell. Write one really good book or even a few good pages and you earn a couple of hundred and seventy-five rubles a week. Write three or four well-written ones and you earn a thousand and eighty-four'
